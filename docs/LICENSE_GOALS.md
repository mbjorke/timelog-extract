# License goals (non-binding intent)

This document explains **why** the repository uses the **Gittan / Timelog Extract License** (`LICENSE`). It does **not** change legal meaning; the `LICENSE` text alone governs.

## What the license is meant to achieve

1. **Sustainable development** — Blueberry Maybe Ab Ltd can keep investing in Gittan without giving away an unrestricted right for large commercial teams to use the Software at scale without contributing back via the sponsorship model in `docs/SPONSORSHIP_TERMS.md`.

2. **Room to evolve terms** — Future releases may ship under **different** license terms (`LICENSE` section 5), so the product can adapt as the market and obligations change, while copies already received under an earlier license remain governed by that version if recipients comply.

3. **Source-available, not OSI “open source”** — Full source is available for inspection and small-team use; **scale use** may require Patreon (or successor) at the mapped tier. That trade-off is intentional.

4. **Alignment with privacy positioning** — Obligations are about **who may use the Software at what scale**, not about harvesting user content on our servers. Default product design remains **local-first**; see `docs/PRIVACY_SECURITY.md`.

5. **Deterministic terms for humans and tools** — Sponsorship Terms are tied to the **revision of the repo you actually have** (commit, tag, or archive), not to a floating branch tip. That keeps obligations **reviewable**: whether the reviewer is a person, CI, or a third-party code-review bot, everyone can open `docs/SPONSORSHIP_TERMS.md` **at that revision** and see the same text. (This is “review-rabbit-friendly”: clear scope, no mystery “latest main” rule.)
